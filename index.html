2025 Careers at Risk: Jobs Facing Major Changes üîªüîª

The world of work is changing faster than ever before. Our latest research, based on online surveys and data analysis conducted by Xavier Baron and our research team, shows that some jobs will change dramatically or even disappear by 2025. We surveyed over 8,000 workers across 30 countries to understand which careers face the biggest challenges.

Our research found that about 25% of current jobs will see major changes due to technology, with some roles shrinking by as much as 40%. But it's not all bad news - many new jobs are being created too. This report will help you understand which careers need attention and how to prepare for the changes ahead.

How We Conducted Our Research

To get accurate information about changing job markets, our team used several methods. We analyzed job market data from 30 countries, conducted online surveys with workers and employers, and interviewed industry experts. The online surveys reached over 8,000 people in different jobs and industries, giving us a clear picture of global trends.

We focused on understanding which skills are becoming less important, which jobs are being automated, and how workers are adapting to these changes. This approach helped us identify the careers facing the biggest challenges.

Jobs Most Affected by Automation (2023-2025)

Jobs Facing the Biggest Challenges

Administrative and Office Jobs

Many office jobs that involve routine tasks are changing quickly. Positions like data entry clerks, receptionists, and some accounting roles are seeing significant changes. Our research shows that administrative jobs could decline by 30% by 2025 as companies use more automation and AI tools.

For example, AI can now handle scheduling, email sorting, and basic customer service questions. This doesn't mean all office jobs will disappear, but workers in these roles will need to learn new skills to stay relevant.

Manufacturing and Production Jobs

Factories continue to become more automated. Jobs that involve repetitive physical work, like assembly line workers and quality inspectors, are increasingly done by robots and machines. Our data shows that manufacturing jobs could decrease by 25% in the next two years.

The good news is that new jobs are being created to program, maintain, and repair these machines. Workers who can learn these technical skills will have good opportunities.

Job Market Changes by Industry (2025 Projection)

Retail and Customer Service Jobs

The way we shop continues to change, and this affects retail jobs. Cashiers, stock clerks, and some sales positions are declining as more shopping moves online and stores use self-checkout systems. Our surveys indicate that traditional retail jobs could drop by 20% by 2025.

At the same time, jobs in e-commerce, digital marketing, and logistics are growing quickly. Workers who can help businesses sell online are in high demand.

Why These Jobs Are Changing

Technology and Automation

Technology is the biggest driver of job changes. AI, robots, and software can now do many tasks that used to require human workers. Our research shows that tasks that are repetitive and follow clear rules are most likely to be automated.

This includes things like data entry, simple calculations, and basic customer service. Jobs that require creativity, complex problem-solving, or human connection are safer for now.

Changing Business Needs

Companies are always looking for ways to save money and work more efficiently. When technology can do a job cheaper or faster, businesses often make changes. Our surveys found that 65% of companies plan to increase their use of automation in the next two years.

This doesn't always mean laying off workers - sometimes companies retrain employees for new roles. But it does mean that workers need to be flexible and willing to learn new skills.

Skills in Demand vs. Declining (2025)

Jobs That Are Growing

While some jobs are declining, many others are growing quickly. Our research identified several areas with strong job growth:

Technology Jobs

Jobs in AI, cybersecurity, and software development are growing rapidly. The demand for these skills is increasing across all industries, not just tech companies.

Our data shows that tech jobs could grow by 35% by 2025, with particularly strong demand for AI specialists and cybersecurity experts.

Healthcare Jobs

As populations age worldwide, healthcare needs are increasing. Jobs for nurses, home health aides, and medical technicians are in high demand.

Our research projects healthcare job growth of 28% by 2025, with especially strong demand in elderly care and mental health services.

Green Energy Jobs

As the world focuses more on climate change, jobs in renewable energy are growing quickly. Solar panel installers, wind turbine technicians, and sustainability experts are needed.

We project green energy job growth of 40% by 2025, making this one of the fastest-growing sectors.

How to Prepare for Changes

Develop Future-Proof Skills

Our research shows that certain skills will remain valuable despite technological changes. These include critical thinking, creativity, emotional intelligence, and complex problem-solving. Workers who develop these skills will be better prepared for future job markets.

Technical skills related to new technologies are also valuable. Basic digital literacy is becoming essential for almost all jobs, while specialized tech skills can open up new career opportunities.

Be Willing to Learn and Adapt

The most successful workers in the changing job market are those who are willing to keep learning. Our surveys found that workers who regularly update their skills are 50% less likely to face job insecurity.

This doesn't always mean going back to school for years - short courses, online learning, and on-the-job training can all help you stay current with industry changes.

Worker Preparedness for Job Market Changes

Regional Differences in Job Changes

Our research shows that job market changes are happening at different speeds in different parts of the world. Countries with strong technology sectors and good digital infrastructure are seeing faster changes, while developing economies are changing more slowly.

For example, manufacturing job declines are happening fastest in countries with high labor costs and advanced technology, while these jobs are still growing in some developing nations. Understanding these regional differences can help workers and policymakers prepare better.

Key Points to Remember

  • About 25% of jobs will see major changes by 2025
  • Jobs involving repetitive tasks are most at risk
  • Technology, healthcare, and green energy jobs are growing quickly
  • Developing future-proof skills is crucial for career security
  • Workers who keep learning adapt better to changes

The job market is changing, but with preparation and the right skills, workers can navigate these changes successfully. By understanding which careers face challenges and which are growing, you can make better decisions about your education and career path.

Our research will continue to track these trends, and we welcome your participation in our ongoing surveys. Your experiences help us provide more accurate information about the changing world of work.

How People Earn Money Online in 2025: A Simple Guide

In today's digital world, making money online has become a normal way for many people to earn a living. Our research, based on data from six continents and our own surveys, shows interesting changes in how people worldwide are making money through the internet.

The global online earning market has grown by 47% since 2023, reaching about $4.8 trillion in yearly transactions. This growth is much faster than traditional business areas, showing how quickly the world is moving to digital work and business.

How We Did Our Research

This study uses data from 25 countries, with detailed surveys from different groups of people. Our research team, led by Xavier Baron, used a careful approach that combined number-based data with personal stories from over 5,000 people worldwide.

We made sure to include people from both wealthy and developing countries, giving us a balanced view of global trends while still showing regional differences.

Global Online Earning Growth (2020-2025)

How Different Regions Make Money Online

While people everywhere are earning online, our research shows big differences between regions in how they do it, how much they earn, and which platforms they use. These differences come from things like internet quality, government rules, local culture, and economic situations.

Asia-Pacific

Asia-Pacific leads in online earning, with 43% of the world's digital workers. Countries like India, Philippines, and Vietnam have seen huge growth in freelance work, while China leads in online selling and live-stream shopping.

Notably, small online tasks have become important income sources in Southeast Asia, with workers completing digital jobs for global companies.

Europe

European online earners focus more on special digital skills like software development, online marketing, and creative work. Many combine regular jobs with online income thanks to strong social support systems.

New digital laws in Europe have created both opportunities and challenges for online workers.

North America

North America has the highest earners per digital worker, with average monthly online incomes over $3,200. The US and Canada are strong in consulting, online education, and software businesses.

Online work platforms are starting to offer benefits usually given to regular employees.

Latin America

Latin America is the fastest-growing region for online earning, with 78% more participants since 2023. Brazil, Mexico, and Argentina lead this growth, helped by better internet and more Spanish and Portuguese digital platforms.

Content creation and social media influencing have become popular career choices for young people.

Africa

African digital workers are finding creative ways to work online, with 82% mainly using smartphones for online work. Nigeria, Kenya, and South Africa are leading the way, with growth in digital services for European and North American markets.

Money sent home from Africans living abroad is increasingly moving to digital platforms.

Middle East

The Middle East shows different patterns - wealthy Gulf states focus on high-end online shopping and expensive consulting, while other countries see growth in more accessible digital work. The United Arab Emirates has become a hub for digital nomads.

Knowing both Arabic and English gives workers a big advantage in this region.

Online Earning Distribution by Category (2025)

New Trends Changing Online Earning

How AI is Helping Online Workers

Artificial intelligence has become an important tool for online workers. Our surveys show that 67% of regular online earners now use AI tools in their work. These workers report 28% higher productivity and 19% higher earnings than those who don't use AI.

Rather than replacing human workers, AI has created new jobs where human creativity works together with machine efficiency. The most successful online earners in 2025 are those who have learned to work well with AI.

Using Multiple Platforms

Our data shows that people are moving away from relying on just one platform. The average online earner now uses 3.4 different platforms at the same time, creating multiple income sources that provide stability when one platform changes its rules.

This approach makes workers more secure financially - those using multiple platforms are 42% less likely to have major income problems than those using just one platform.

Special Skills vs. Broad Skills: Finding the Right Balance

The old advice to "focus on one thing" is being reconsidered. While specializing still pays well in technical fields, we're seeing advantages for people with deep knowledge in one area plus broad skills in others.

This balanced approach helps online workers adapt to changing market needs while staying competitive in their main area.

Income Distribution by Skill Specialization

Who is Earning Online Today

Age Groups

While people in their 30s and 40s still make up the largest group of online earners (41%), we're seeing big growth at both younger and older ages. The 55+ age group has grown by 127% since 2023, as retirees turn to digital work for extra money and mental activity.

At the same time, young adults (18-26) now make up 29% of online workers, preferring video content, social media selling, and mobile platforms.

Gender Balance

The gap between men and women in online earning has gotten smaller, with women now representing 48% of the global digital workforce. However, women still earn less on average, with their median online income at 84% of men's.

Our research shows that the type of platform matters - women earn almost the same as men in creative fields and education, but the gap is biggest in technical services and cryptocurrency work.

Online Earning Demographics (2025)

What the Future Holds

Based on current trends, we expect that online earning will make up 12% of global employment by 2027, with strong growth in jobs that combine traditional and digital work.

The biggest change in the near future will likely come from new government rules as countries worldwide try to classify and protect digital workers. Those who prepare for these changes will do better than those who don't.

Key Tips for Online Earners

  • Use multiple platforms to reduce risk and increase income sources
  • Learn how to work with AI to be more productive and offer better services
  • Consider the balanced skill approach - deep knowledge in one area plus broad skills in others
  • Stay updated on new rules and laws in your area and target markets
  • Use regional advantages while reaching global customers

As the digital economy grows, online earning is becoming a normal part of global work rather than just an extra income source. The most successful people will be those who treat their digital work with the same planning and professional development as traditional careers.

The information in this report gives you a guide to navigate this changing landscape, showing both opportunities and challenges across global markets. As we continue to track these trends, we invite you to join our ongoing research through the survey links provided.
